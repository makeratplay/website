2026 Marching Band Props

Mobile stair & slide platforms

This page tracks the 2026 marching band field props: mobile units that combine a short staircase, a top working platform, and a slide on a wheeled metal frame. Units can be used alone or arranged so platforms meet at a corner for a larger performance footprint.

The design is still being validated with students and staff (slide angle, transitions, handles, and materials). Detailed build logs will land here and on the Maker At Play blog as the season progresses.

Backdrops & frames · Trailer · Front stages · Load plan · Progress

Concept & specifications (working)

Current targets from design review and the first prototype (subject to change):

  • Footprint: about 7 ft wide by 4 ft deep on casters.
  • Platform height: roughly 69 in. from the ground to the top deck (just under 6 ft with typical wheel height).
  • Stairs: five rises to the platform; stair tread width about 2 ft.
  • Top platform: about 2 ft deep, shared by the stairs and slide entry.
  • Slide: plywood ramp; final surface material and angle TBD after student trials.
  • Sides: coroplast panels (blue in renders) over a metal frame; 1 in. tube handles on the sides near the 4 ft level for maneuvering.
  • Mobility: locking casters at the corners; wheel inset from corners to reduce interference when props sit side by side.

Budget and fleet size (for example, how many units and how they pack into trucks) are being coordinated with the program. A rough per-unit materials estimate has been on the order of mid–three figures including metal, coroplast sheet, slide materials, and wheels, before final scope is locked.

Backdrops: Media Frames & 10 mm Coroplast

We’re returning to the same media-frame approach with 10 mm coroplast for the backdrops this year. This is my third iteration on solving the attachment problem without relying on Velcro. I’ve never been a fan—it behaves more like tape than a structural solution, tends to fail under stress, and often needs mid-season replacement.

This season, I’m planning to add a piece of angle iron along the bottom of each frame to create a channel for the coroplast to sit in. This should be more stable than last year’s approach of bolting along the bottom edge.

I also want to introduce a mid-frame rail to provide a solid mounting point across the center. Attaching only at the edges left the panels unsupported, which caused misalignment and visible gaps at the seams. While I’d prefer to avoid it, a small strip of Velcro on the top rail may still be the most practical way to control the top edge, assuming the rest of the structure carries the load.

Trailer: Racks, Frames, and Coroplast Storage

Bringing the backdrops back also means rethinking how everything rides in the trailer. This year, I plan to build a dedicated rack system so each component has a defined place instead of competing for floor space.

Since this is also my final year as props lead, and assuming the media-frame props will continue to be used in future seasons, it makes sense to build a formal, repeatable storage solution within the 20 ft trailer that will provide long-term value beyond this year.

I also want to upgrade the coroplast storage by improving how sheets are secured to the shelf or trailer wall. The goal is to keep them flat and reduce the risk of warping during transport.

Front Stages

We’re reusing and expanding last season’s front stages. The plan is to add one additional 4 × 8 ft section, bringing us to four total, and then stage them in pairs on each side of the front ensemble.

I also plan to build two simple sets of steps that can be placed on the ground behind the stages to provide easier on/off access. For transport on and off the field, the steps would ride on top of the stages.

Load Planning: Trucks & Trailer

I still need to map out the full load plan, including stair props, front stages, media frames with coroplast, and front-screen carts across the two box trucks and the 20 ft trailer.

The goal is to understand how much capacity remains in the box trucks for overflow electronics that would otherwise go on the semi, so we can eliminate guesswork on contest weekends.

All told, this is shaping up to be a big props year.

Progress notes

High-level milestones; fuller write-ups will be linked from here as they are published.

  • Earlier 2026 — Initial mockups from program direction; iteration toward height under 6 ft at the deck and guard-access ideas (e.g. storage for flag poles — placement TBD).
