Welcome to the 2024 Marching Band Props Project

Join us on this exciting journey of creating an immersive visual experience for our 2024 marching band show.

Revealed in April 2024, this year's show "It's About Time" transforms the field into a mesmerizing graveyard of timepieces. Our vision brings to life an array of clocks, hourglasses, pocket watches, and watch gears, creating the illusion of these timekeeping artifacts being partially buried in the field.

The centerpiece of our design is a magnificent sundial that serves as both a symbolic timekeeper and a functional performance space. This innovative structure allows soloists to ascend and perform, while also providing a dramatic staging area below.

Concept & Design

The initial concept sketch laid out an ambitious vision for our 2024 show. The design incorporates:

  • 16 Timepiece Props: Ranging from 6x6 feet to 12x12 feet, these pieces will create our field of time
  • 22-foot Sundial: A centerpiece that doubles as a performance platform
  • 180 feet of Front Drops: 90 feet on each side of the front ensemble

Using Coroplast printing technology, we're creating a set design that transforms the field into a mesmerizing graveyard of timepieces. Each prop is carefully designed to create depth and dimension, giving the illusion of partially buried artifacts.

Technical Specifications

  • Pointer Length: 12 feet from center
  • Width: 3 feet
  • Height: 6 feet from ground
  • Platform: 3x3 feet, 4 feet elevation
  • Features: Internal staircase, modular design for transport

The sundial stage was our first major focus in the construction process. This centerpiece needed to be both visually striking and functionally sound, supporting performers while maintaining the show's temporal theme.

Key design considerations included:

  • Safe and stable performance platform
  • Modular construction for trailer transport
  • Efficient assembly/disassembly process
  • Integration with ground tarp design

Front view of initial 3D design

Side view showing stair design

The initial design featured stairs with a 6-inch rise and 12-inch run, providing comfortable access to the performance platform. Using Fusion 360, we created detailed 3D models to validate dimensions and visualize the final structure before beginning construction.

Sideline Screens

The band previously used sideline screens made out of PVC, measuring 4x10 feet. They were assembled using dry-fit PVC fittings — 90-degree elbows for the top corners and three-way elbows for the legs and bottom corners. Moving and setting up these screens has always been a challenge. Due to the fixed 90-degree legs, it's difficult to nest the screens or move them on a cart, and the legs tend to come loose frequently.

My goal has been to replace these PVC frames with metal frames featuring kickstand-style legs that fold into the frame. I've seen other schools use these, and they appear to be much easier to set up and move around. It's also part of the plan to build dedicated carts for transporting the sideline screens. We'll use 4x8 sheets of 4mm Coroplast for the sideline screen artwork. Given these dimensions, it made sense to build the frames to match the 4x8 size. Building 22 screens will provide 88 feet of coverage on each side of the front ensemble. Additional design considerations include ensuring that the legs have a stop when opened, allowing quick and uniform setup for all the screens. I wanted a design with no loose parts and no assembly required. I had seen a few similar designs online retailing for around $300 each. However, I can source the metal for about $50 per screen — and band dad labor is free! There will also be a few nuts, bolts, and some spray paint needed to complete them. The carts will cost about $100 in metal, and we plan to reuse wheels from a previous year's prop build. We are building two carts; while all 22 screens could fit on a single cart, that much weight would be too heavy to manage safely.

Back Props

There will be a total of 16 back props, each coroplast-printed with different timepieces:

  • 4 extra-large (12x12)
  • 4 large (10x10)
  • 4 medium (8x8)
  • 4 small (6x6)

Each prop was assigned a spot number and color-coded to its frame to make assembly easier and faster.

To support the back props we are building Expandable Media Frames that expand from 4x4 feet to 10x10 feet.

4 foot by 4 foot setup

7 foot by 7 foot setup

10 foot by 10 foot setup

However, we abandoned this idea after building a prototype frame and not being satisfied with how it turned out. Expanding and contracting the frame was not easy, and it seemed more practical to build fixed-size frames instead. So, that is what we did. We built seven fixed-size media frames with wheels and nine fixed frames out of EMT that sit on the ground.

Timepiece Props

The field design features 16 unique timepiece props, each carefully designed to create a surreal landscape of time. These props include:

  • Vintage pocket watches
  • Ornate clock faces
  • Mechanical gears and movements
  • Traditional hourglasses

Each prop is constructed using a combination of:

  • Coroplast printed panels for the visual elements
  • EMT conduit framework for structural support
  • Custom mounting hardware for assembly/disassembly
  • Reinforced bases for stability during performance

Project Completion

The "It's About Time" props project represents a significant achievement in marching band prop design and construction. Through careful planning, innovative engineering, and dedicated teamwork, we successfully created:

  • A fully functional 22-foot sundial stage that serves both as a visual centerpiece and performance platform
  • 16 unique timepiece props that transform the field into a mesmerizing landscape
  • 180 feet of front drops that frame the performance space
  • A modular design that allows for efficient transportation and setup

The props not only met the technical requirements but also enhanced the show's theme and provided a stunning visual experience for both performers and audience members.

Key Achievements

  • Completed all props on schedule
  • Successfully fit within transportation constraints
  • Maintained durability throughout the season
  • Received positive feedback from show designers

Future Considerations

  • Documented assembly procedures for future reference
  • Identified potential design improvements
  • Created maintenance guidelines
  • Established best practices for future prop projects
